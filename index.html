Welcome & Overview

What is the Build-a-Tech Startup Project?

Students work in teams to design a fictional tech startup that offers a product or service. You'll incorporate at least three IT concepts and create a pitch for the class, Shark Tank-style.

What You'll Be Doing

Over the course of this project, you'll brainstorm ideas, select IT concepts to incorporate, design mockups, and prepare a compelling presentation to pitch your startup idea to the class.

Learning Objectives

  • Apply IT knowledge creatively (hardware, software, networking, AI, cybersecurity, etc.)
  • Develop communication and teamwork skills
  • Practice pitching and public speaking
  • Gain experience with design tools (Canva, Figma, or Google Slides)

Why This Matters

This project helps you develop real-world skills in technology innovation, collaboration, and presentation that are highly valued in the workplace.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Day 1

Team Formation & Brainstorming

  • Form teams of 3-4 students
  • Brainstorm startup ideas that solve real problems
  • Begin discussing potential IT concepts to include
Day 2

Planning Your Product

  • Define your product/service in detail
  • Select at least 3 IT concepts to incorporate
  • Outline your business model and target audience
Day 3

Visual Design & Pitch Development

  • Design your company logo
  • Create product mockups or interface designs
  • Start building your pitch deck presentation
Day 4

Presentation Preparation

  • Finalize your pitch deck
  • Practice your presentation as a team
  • Prepare for potential questions from the "sharks"
Day 5

Shark Tank Presentations & Awards

  • Deliver your 5-7 minute pitch
  • Answer questions from the sharks
  • Participate in peer voting for best pitches

Submission Details

Due Dates

  • Pitch Deck Submission: Due the day before presentations
  • Final Presentations: Will take place on the scheduled presentation day

How to Submit

Submit your pitch deck (Google Slides, PowerPoint, or PDF) through the class submission portal. Make sure all team members' names are included.

Submission Tips

  • Double-check that your presentation displays correctly
  • Include any mockup files or additional resources as separate attachments
  • Submit at least 12 hours before the deadline to avoid technical issues

Presentation Day & Awards

Shark Tank Format

Your teacher and selected students will play the role of "Sharks." After each pitch:

  • Sharks will ask questions about the tech or business side
  • They may critique the viability and innovation of the idea
  • Teams should be prepared to defend their concept

Judging Criteria

Innovation (25%)

Originality and creativity of the idea

IT Implementation (25%)

Effective use of IT concepts

Viability (20%)

Business model and market potential

Presentation (20%)

Clarity, organization, and delivery

Visual Design (10%)

Quality of mockups and presentation design

Award Categories

Best Overall Startup

Most Innovative Concept

Best Technical Implementation

Most Viable Business

Best Design & Branding

Presentation Tips

  • Keep your pitch concise and engaging
  • Practice your timing to stay within 5-7 minutes
  • Be prepared to answer technical questions about your IT concepts
  • Show enthusiasm for your product/service
  • Make eye contact with your audience
